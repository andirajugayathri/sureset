Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some commonly asked questions about SureSet Securement. If you're looking for additional information, please contact us here.

↗ Is SureSet compatible with all forms of PIV catheters?
⌄

Yes, SureSet is designed to be compatible with all standard PIV catheters. The device accommodates various catheter sizes and types commonly used in clinical settings.

↗ Is the SureSet Peripheral IV stabilization device appropriate for all patients with PIVs?
⌄

SureSet is suitable for most patients requiring PIV stabilization. However, individual patient assessment should be conducted to ensure appropriateness based on skin condition and clinical needs.

↗ Any special tips or instructions for fitting the SureSet PIV stabilization device onto the catheter?
⌄

Follow the provided instructions for use. Ensure the catheter is properly inserted and secured before applying the SureSet device. The device should fit snugly without causing discomfort to the patient.

↗ Is SureSet reusable?
⌄

No, SureSet is a single-use device designed for one-time application to maintain sterility and optimal performance.

↗ Is SureSet Latex free?
⌄

Yes, SureSet is latex-free to minimize the risk of allergic reactions in sensitive patients.

↗ If I want to change the tubing, does the device need to be removed?
⌄

No, tubing changes can typically be performed without removing the SureSet device, allowing for continued stabilization during routine care.

↗ Do I have to use a special skin prep with the SureSet stabilization device?
⌄

Follow your facility's standard skin preparation protocols. SureSet is compatible with commonly used skin prep solutions.

↗ Does SureSet have a version for children and infants?
⌄

Please contact us for information about pediatric sizing options and recommendations for use with children and infants.

↗ Can the SureSet device be placed over insertion sites other than the hand?
⌄

Yes, SureSet can be used on various PIV insertion sites including the hand, forearm, and other appropriate locations as determined by clinical assessment.
